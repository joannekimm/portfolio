Grainjar cereal company

Building an e-commerce website for a cereal company

Context

7 weeks, Spring 2021
Spring 2021 SIAT project (SFU)

Team & role

Clare Huang, Sophia Wang
My role: high-fidelity mockup, wireframing, prototyping, site-mapping, style guide, HTML/CSS & JavaScript

Overview

For my IAT 339 class, I was given the task to prototype a responsive e-commerce website for a desired fictional company. My team decided to base our project on a cereal company where customers can buy their favorite organic and ethically-sourced in BULK through our website.

User goals

We established some of the user goals we wanted to consider and implement when prototyping our website.

  • Quickly find what they are searching for
  • Easily decide whether or not they like the companyâ€™s proposition to the customer
  • Intuitively navigate through the website
  • Identify consistent interactive cues throughout the website pages

Client goals

  • The client should be able to showcase their products to customers clearly and effectively
  • The client should be able to communicate their ethos through design
  • Allow the user to get to anywhere on the site through their current page location
  • Communicate a clear sustainable and organic image

Process

1. Moodboard

My team started off our design process by each creating a unique moodboard to consolidate a theme, color palette and mood we desired to establish in the website. To reflect the sustainable and oragnic image of the company, I chose a neutral color palette with brown and orange accents.

My teammate Clare had a divergent moodboard with a grudgy and bold theme. My team decided to move forward with my idea and Clare's as a secondary choice with goals to incorporate the darker theme somewhere in the website.

2. Style guide prototype

Next, we had to prototype a style guide on Figma preliminary to individually building a final brand style guide for grainjar on HTML/CSS. To get a better sense of what style guides look like, we had to do additional research on brand style guides for websites.

3. Sitemap

My team created a sitemap to represent the relationships between pages and establish a hierarchy to efficiently sort pages according to their function. The "shopping cart" pages are diffrentiated in color from the rest of the pages.

4. Crazy 8 Wireframes

After the initial ideation phase, my team had a mini crazy 8 design spring asynchronously. We tried to explore as many home page and products page layouts in a short time frame.

4. Wireframes

After brainstorming through quick sketches on paper, we moved on to creating high-fidelity wireframes on Figma. The most time consuming part was designing the checkout pages as they needed to be as concise as possible.

5. High-fidelity mockup

I explored different layouts by prototyping low/high-fidelity mockups on Figma and went through 3-4 iterations to establish a unique style for the website. One of the feedbacks I got from my TA in one of the crit sessions is the fact that my prototype looked too simple. In the following iteration, I tried to add more dynamic to the overall website.

Problem space

While brainstorming, we realized that if we sort according to their type and ingredients, many cereals would overlap in multiple categories, creating ambiguity.

Solutions

As a result, we took an unconventional approach with sorting cereal. We organized them by different occasions rather than based on the different types and flavours.

Unique categories

  • Different banners
  • Different background colors
  • Unique layouts for each page
  • Unique set of curated cereal

Designing a brand style guide

Each member of the team created a brand style guide unique to the website. It was important to create a reference tool in order to maintain consistency and facilitate the collaboration process when it comes to actually building the website. I built the style guide as well as UI modules such as the main navigation bar and product detail module using HTML/CSS. I included code snippets below each section as a reference.
